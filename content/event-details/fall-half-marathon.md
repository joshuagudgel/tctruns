## About This Race

Our premier fall event featuring three distance options through historic downtown. The half marathon course showcases the city's best architecture and landmarks, while the shorter distances offer perfect introduction to downtown racing.

This race has been a community tradition for over 15 years, bringing together runners of all abilities to celebrate fitness and our beautiful historic city.

## Course Information

### Half Marathon Course (13.1 Miles)

- **Miles 1-3:** Historic district with heritage building tours
- **Miles 4-8:** Riverfront greenway with scenic overlooks
- **Miles 9-13.1:** Return through downtown with crowd support
- **Surface:** 90% paved roads, 10% improved greenway trail
- **Elevation:** Rolling hills with 400ft total gain

### 10K Course (6.2 Miles)

- Historic district and riverfront portions of the half marathon
- Best highlights of the downtown area
- Finish at the same downtown location as other distances

### 5K Course (3.1 Miles)

- Best highlights of the downtown historic core
- Family-friendly route with minimal elevation
- Perfect introduction to downtown racing

### Aid Stations

- **Every 2 miles** with water, sports drink, and snacks
- **Mile 8 aid station** (half marathon only) includes energy gels
- **Finish line** celebration with local food vendors

## Drop Bag Service

_Available for Half Marathon participants only_

### Drop Bag Details

- **Check bags** at Friday expo or race morning (no check after 7:15 AM)
- **Bags transported** to mile 8 riverfront aid station
- **Retrieved at finish line** after race completion
- **Maximum size:** Standard drawstring bag or small backpack

### Drop Bag Guidelines

- **Label clearly** with bib number and name in permanent marker
- **No valuables, electronics, or perishables** - we are not responsible for lost items
- **Suggested contents:** Extra nutrition, clothing layers, personal items
- **Unclaimed bags** held for 30 days then donated to charity

### Drop Bag Tips

- Use a disposable bag if possible
- Include contact information inside bag as backup
- Check weather forecast and pack accordingly

## Spectator & Crew Information

### Best Spectator Access Points

- **Start/Finish:** Downtown Plaza (best viewing and parking)
- **Mile 3:** Historic Square (limited parking, public transit access)
- **Mile 8:** Riverfront Park (aid station access, food trucks)
- **Mile 11:** City Bridge (great photo opportunity, easy access)

### Crew Guidelines

- **No crew aid** between official aid stations per USATF rules
- **Stay on sidewalks** and designated spectator areas
- **Follow volunteer and police directions** for safety
- **Cheering encouraged!** Your support means everything to runners

### Spectator Tips

- Arrive early for parking at popular viewing spots
- Bring chairs for comfort during longer waits
- Check our race app for live runner tracking
- Join the finish line celebration after your runner finishes
