## About This Race

First race in our popular summer trail series. Challenging terrain with beautiful scenic views and two distance options to accommodate different fitness levels.

This series celebrates trail running in our local mountains and builds community among outdoor enthusiasts throughout the summer season.

## Course Information

### 10K Course

- **Technical single-track trails** with root and rock sections
- **800ft elevation gain** over rolling terrain
- **Creek crossing at mile 2.5** (may require wading in spring)
- **Spectacular overlook at mile 4** with mountain views
- **Aid stations:** Water and electrolytes at miles 2.5 and 5

### 5K Course

- **First 2.5 miles** of the 10K route then return
- **400ft elevation gain**, less technical sections
- **Creek crossing and return** - shorter but still scenic
- **Perfect for beginners** to trail running

### Trail Conditions

- **Surface:** Natural trail surfaces with some rocky sections
- **Terrain:** Mix of single-track and fire roads
- **Difficulty:** Moderate to challenging (10K), Easy to moderate (5K)
- **Weather dependent:** Trail conditions may vary

## Registration Details

### What to Expect

- **Trail running experience recommended** for 10K distance
- **5K suitable for hiking/trail beginners** looking to try trail racing
- All finishers receive a **custom trail series medal**
- **Technical t-shirts available** for $15 add-on at registration

### Recommended Gear

- **Trail running shoes strongly recommended** (no road shoes!)
- **Hydration pack or water bottles** suggested for longer distance
- **Weather-appropriate clothing** (mountain weather changes quickly)
- **Optional:** Trekking poles allowed for steep sections

### Race Day Essentials

- Arrive early for limited parking
- Bring layers - mountain mornings can be cool
- Pack out all trash - leave no trace principles apply

## Spectator & Crew Information

### Spectator Access Points

- **Start/Finish:** Trailhead parking area (best viewing)
- **Mile 2.5:** Creek crossing (accessible via fire road)
- **No vehicle access** to overlook area due to trail terrain

### Crew Guidelines

- **Stay on marked trails only** - respect wilderness areas
- **Yield to runners** on single-track sections
- **No littering** - pack out all trash per Leave No Trace
- **Follow volunteer directions** for safety

## Directions & Parking

### Trailhead Location

**Mountain View Trail System**  
Forest Service Road 142

### Driving Directions

- **From downtown:** Take Highway 9 North for 15 miles
- **Turn right** on Forest Service Road 142
- **Follow signs** for 3 miles to trailhead parking
- **Road conditions:** Paved all the way, suitable for all vehicles

### Parking Options

- **Trailhead lot:** 50 spaces (arrive early - fills up fast!)
- **Overflow shuttle** from Main Street Park & Ride
- **Shuttle service** runs every 15 minutes starting at 6:30 AM
- **Carpooling encouraged** due to limited mountain parking

### Important Notes

- **No facilities** at trailhead - use restrooms at overflow lot
- **Cell service limited** in mountain areas
- **Weather updates** posted on our social media morning of race
